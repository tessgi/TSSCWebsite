# TESS Program G08149

Title: Orbital Dynamics As A Probe Of Tidal Heating In Rocky Planets

PI: Greklek-Mckeon, Michael - California Institute Of Technology

Type: SMALL

Summary: We will use TESS Cycle 8 data to measure orbital eccentricities and tidal heat fluxes in rocky planets through transit timing variations (TTVs). Even tiny eccentricities (<0.01) for close-in planets can generate significant tidal heating, affecting interior/atmospheric composition and habitability. We will analyze 3 systems already targeted by JWST that lack precise tidal heating constraints. Combining Cycle 8 data with archival data will extend the TTV baseline by 2-5 years, breaking model degeneracies and yielding precise eccentricities. This will provide the first ever sample of tidal heat flux measurements for rocky planets with atmospheric characterization beyond TRAPPIST-1, enhancing our understanding of how tidal heating affects small planet composition, evolution, and habitability.
