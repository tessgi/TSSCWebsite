ExoFOP-TESS now online

Date Tue 01 August 2017 By Elisa Quintana

ExoFOP-TESS is a web-based service developed and operated by the NASA Exoplanet Science Institute (NExScI) and the NASA Exoplanet Archive to optimize resources and facilitate collaboration in follow-up studies of targets observed by TESS. ExoFOP serves as a repository for community-gathered follow-up data by allowing upload and display of data and derived astrophysical parameters, and has been widely used by the Kepler and K2 Follow-Up Observation Programs.

In the initial release of ExoFOP-TESS, overview pages for each of the 10.8 million stars in the TESS Candidate Target list (CTL) have been created. These pages contain data from the TESS Input Catalog (TIC) Version 5, including coordinates, magnitudes, stellar parameters, and planet parameters (if available). The pages also contain external links to other archives, and all content can be viewed without logging in. The ability to upload data and parameters and to search the CTL targets will be coming soon.

See our ExoFOP-TESS page for more details.
