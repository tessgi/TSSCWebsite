Citation Guidelines Table

Here we list how to cite some of the TESS data products.

Suggested References:

  • FFIs: Jenkins et al. (2016)
  • SAP_FLUX light curves: Twicken et al. (2010), Jenkins et al. (2016), Morris et al. (2020)
  • PDC_SAP light curves: Stumpe et al. (2012, 2014), Smith et al. (2012)
  • TESS-SPOC FFI light curves: Caldwell et al. (2020)

Suggested acknowledgments:

“We acknowledge the use of TESS data processed by the TESS Science Processing Operations Center at NASA Ames Research Center, which are publicly available from the Mikulski Archive for Space Telescopes (MAST). Funding for the TESS mission is provided by NASA's Science Mission directorate.”

Suggested references:

  • QLP light curves: Huang et al. (2020a), (Huang et al. (2020b), Kunimoto et al. (2021), Kunimoto et al. (2022)

Suggested acknowledgments:

“We acknowledge the use of TESS High Level Science Products (HLSP) produced by the Quick-Look Pipeline (QLP) at the TESS Science Office at MIT, which are publicly available from the Mikulski Archive for Space Telescopes (MAST). Funding for the TESS mission is provided by NASA's Science Mission directorate.”

Suggested reference:

  • TICA FFIs: Fausnaugh et al. (2020)

Suggested acknowledgments:

"We acknowledge the use of TESS High Level Science Products (HLSP) produced by the TESS Image CAlibrator Full Frame Image (TICA) pipeline at the TESS Science Office at MIT, which are publicly available from the Mikulski Archive for Space Telescopes (MAST). Funding for the TESS mission is provided by NASA's Science Mission directorate."

Suggested text that can be adapted as necessary:

“The SPOC conducted a transit search of Sector X on day/month/year with an adaptive, noise-compensating matched filter (Jenkins 2002; Jenkins et al. 2010, 2020), producing a TCE for which an initial limb-darkened transit model was fitted (Li et al. 2019) and a suite of diagnostic tests were conducted to help make or break the planetary nature of the signal (Twicken et al. 2018). The transit signature was also detected in a search of Full Frame Image (FFI) data by the Quick Look Pipeline (QLP) at MIT (Huang et al. 2020a, b, Kunimoto et al. 2021, Kunimoto et al. 2022). The TESS Science Office (TSO) reviewed the vetting information and issued an alert on day/month/year (Guerrero et al. 2021). The signal was repeatedly recovered as additional observations were made in Sectors x, y, z, etc., and the transit signature passed all the diagnostic tests presented in the Data Validation reports. The difference image centroiding test indicated that host star is located within x ± i arcsec of the source of the transit signal.”

Please feel free to contact the mission team if you have questions about the data products used in your paper and/or wish to invite a representative coauthor who can assist you with interpretation and analysis.

  • SPOC contact: Jon Jenkins, jon.jenkins@nasa.gov
  • QLP contact: Glen Petitpas, petitpas@mit.edu
  • TESS help desk: tesshelp@bigbang.gsfc.nasa.gov

SPOC team members can assist you with interpretation and analysis of the SPOC light curves and DV products, including:

  • Assistance with interpreting and analyzing SPOC data products,
  • Running Data Validation in a forced ephemeris mode to generate DV reports for any additional candidates identified through independent transit searches to help bolster confidence in the detection and planetary classification or to help identify issues with such candidates,
  • Providing alternative analysis of SPOC data to recovered transits gapped in the PDC_SAP flux in the presence of scattered light features, and
  • Analyzing any Year 2 data that may be affected by the sky background bias issue and providing the impact to the imputed planetary radii and guidance for correcting this effect.

Note: the SPOC Data Validation (DV) reports can be found via the MAST Data Portal and the processing date is located at the bottom of the 1st page of the DV mini-reports and summary reports. Note that the difference image test conducted by the SPOC can typically locate the source of the transits to within 2.5 arcsec for reasonably strong transit signals. We recommending using the ticoffset-rm entry from the DV report (located in the bottom right of the summary report or 1st page of the mini-report), which indicates the source offset from the catalog location in arcsec and the significance, in sigma. Dividing the source offset by the significance yields the 1-sigma uncertainty in arcsec.

The out-of-transit (OOT) offset can be compromised in crowded fields and is not recommended as the TIC-offset is usually more reliable. The difference image centroiding test complements high resolution imaging and is usually available well before any follow-up observations are conducted. For multisector searches from June 2025 onwards the joint TIC offset, TicOffset-jnt, is recommended as it is significantly more accurate than the TicOffset-rm metric. The TOI alert date can be found in the TOI Catalog at tev.mit.edu along with QLP, FAINT and SPOC DV reports for each TOI (note the TOI catalog does not include all SPOC DV reports, all of which can be found at MAST).
